## Research

My research is broadly motivated by **combating corruption** — both in the physical world and in AI systems.

### 🛰 Generalizable Slum Segmentation via Remote Sensing
Informal settlements lack reliable geospatial datasets, making global poverty mapping difficult. I build datasets and models that generalize across regions **without local labels** — using Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) to capture regional visual variation and Test-Time Adaptation (TTA) for zero-shot transfer to unseen geographies.

### 🎲 LLM Agent Corruption via Bribery Game Theory *(ongoing)*
LLMs are increasingly deployed in public-facing roles as neutral arbiters, yet can behave self-interestedly under certain conditions. I use a **bribery game framework** — where rational agents have no incentive to deviate — to isolate when and why models engage in corrupt behavior, extending to LLM–human interactions in governance-like settings.
